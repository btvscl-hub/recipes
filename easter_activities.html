Grow you own sunflower from a seed!

Try to grow your own sunflower by following the instructions provided below.

You can buy all the things you need from a garden centre:

  • Sunflower seeds
  • Small growing pot
  • Soil
  • Larger growing pot
  • Watering can

Sunflowers need plenty of sunlight and good soil to grow well; and once they have started to grow, they also need water regularly.

Put some damp soil in a small pot so it almost comes up to the top.

Then poke your finger in the soil to make a hole for your seed.

Drop a sunflower seed into the hole.

Cover it with a little soil.

Spray a bit of water on top of the soil.

Put your pot in a sunny place – a bright windowsill would be perfect.

Spray the soil with a bit more water whenever it feels dry.

Please be patient and keep your pot in a sunny place, and keep watering the pot whenever it gets dry – it may take 1 or 2 weeks before your plant starts to grow!

Keep on spraying your new young growing plant with water every day.

When your plant grows too big for your first small pot, as shown, you might need to plant it in a bigger flowerpot.

Fill up your new larger pot with soil about three quarters of the way up, as shown.

Carefully get hold of your sunflower plant from your old pot, and grab all of it (with roots & soil).

Now put your plant into the new larger pot, making sure that you bed down all the roots properly.

You’ll have to wait for quite a while (two months) before you see any flowers – but we’re hoping that you will see your sunflower showing in June this year!
